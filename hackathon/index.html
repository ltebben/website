Hackathon Organizer

Spring 2017 and Spring 2018 I was the logistics director of RevolutionUC, UC's student-run hackathon. A hackathon is a chance for students everywhere to get together and make something with technology. They are typically 24 hour events in which students form teams, come up with a "hack," and build a working prototype to demo to judges. RevolutionUC is completely free for students, and we provide snacks, meals, swag, and prizes.

The logistics team was responsible for a variety of things, including deciding on what meals and swag to get and placing the orders, working with the univeristy to register the rooms and get 24-hour access, ensuring that the event adheres to the university's minors on campus guidelines, and planning and organizing judging. As direcor, I led weekly discussion-based meetings for a team of 6, delegating tasks as necessary. My team did an excellent job and the Spring 2018 hackathon was the smoothest one yet (logistically).

Leading the logistics team has been a wonderful experience for me. It has given me the opportunity to grow my leadership skills while doing something I love. I've been an organizer for RevolutionUC for the past 3 years and it has been great to watch it grow and to grow alongside it. For my senior year, I will be stepping back into an adviory role and one of the younger students will be taking my place as logistics director. I am excited to help them grow into this role and put on a great event!
